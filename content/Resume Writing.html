Lesson Objectives

Create a resume 
Identify transferable skills 
Practice writing achievement statements 
Assess your personal strengths 

About

This lesson is designed to walk first year students through the process of creating a professional resume. Use the following resources to review or create your resume.

Introduction

Watch the following video about writing strong resumes from Harvard Extension School. This video also briefly discusses cover letters. Although it uses college graduate examples, the overall lessons regarding accomplishment statements are important to keep in mind as you being writing your own resume.

Next, read this article that talks about what you can put on your resume if you’ve never held a formal job or don’t have experience in the field you want to obtain a job in. 
Read: https://www.thebalance.com/first-resume-example-with-no-work-experience-2063278

Transferable Skills

As mentioned in the article, one of the keys to writing your first resume is to look at what skills you do have and how they might translate into a professional setting. As first-year students, you likely have some experience with clubs and activities from high school, and maybe even some volunteer experience. This page will help you identify skills you definitely picked up from those experiences and help you articulate how those skills apply to your career goals.

Handout: Princeton University 
https://careerservices.princeton.edu/undergraduate-students/resumes-letters-online-profiles/resumes/transferable-skills 

Writing Accomplishment Statements

Accomplishment (or achievement) statements are direct, quantifiable sentences that clearly explain to a potential employer how your previous experience qualifies you for a potential role at a new company.

Handout: Princeton University 
https://careerservices.princeton.edu/undergraduate-students/resumes-letters-online-profiles/resumes/accomplishment-statements
